Summary

Mapped micro-slopes and surface flow using elevation rasters to locate persistent ponding and premature wear. Built a scoring model ranking interventions by severity, traffic exposure, and estimated repair cost.

  • Flow accumulation exposed hidden low points
  • Prioritized fixes by cost/impact
  • Reusable workflow for new segments

Problem

Frequent ponding and surface degradation increased safety risk and maintenance cost. Historical fixes were reactive due to limited insight into surface flow paths.

Approach

Processed survey elevation data in Python; generated slope vectors and flow accumulation; validated hotspots against incident logs; exported styled layers for field teams.

Architecture / Stack

Python (Pandas, NumPy), QGIS, raster I/O; HTML/PDF reporting.

Results

  • 35% reduction in drainage-related rework in two quarters
  • SOP documented and reused across new segments
