Summary

We transformed raw survey elevations into actionable drainage intelligence. By generating slope rasters, flow accumulation, and catchment boundaries, we identified micro-depressions and flow bottlenecks that consistently created ponding and premature surface wear.

A severity model combined traffic exposure, rainfall events, maintenance history, and cost-to-fix. This produced a ranked intervention list that crews could execute by section, raising safety and cutting rework. The process is fully repeatable for new road segments.

  • Micro-slope + flow analysis pinpointed high-risk zones
  • Prioritized fixes by cost/impact and accessibility
  • Reusable SOP for future segments and audits

Problem

Frequent ponding and surface degradation raised safety risks and maintenance costs. Fixes were reactive due to limited visibility into true flow paths.

Approach

Processed DEM/survey data in Python; produced slope vectors and flow accumulation; validated hotspots against incident logs and rainfall events; exported styled layers to guide crews.

Architecture / Stack

Python (Pandas, NumPy, rasterio), QGIS for cartography; HTML/PDF reporting; versioned pipeline for reruns.

Results

  • 35% reduction in drainage-related rework within two quarters
  • Improved crew planning and safety interventions
